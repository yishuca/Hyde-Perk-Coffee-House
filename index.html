Lightswitch Riddle

There are 100 lightswitches "upstairs."
There are 100 lightbulbs "downstairs."
They all need to be labeled correspondingly.

Each light switch goes to 1 lightbulb.

You start upstairs, with the switches off. You can flip on/off and label however many switches you want,
then check downstairs to see which light(s) turned on, and label those.

This counts as one trip, from upstairs to downstairs.

What is the least amount of trips it would take to be able to accurately
label every lightswitch to every lightbulb?
